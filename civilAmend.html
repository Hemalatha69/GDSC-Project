Updates about civil cases

Civil cases involve legal disputes between individuals, organizations, or entities, typically seeking compensation, resolution, or a specific remedy rather than criminal penalties. These cases are diverse and can cover a wide range of legal issues.

Corporate cases

Shah Bano Case (1985)

The Shah Bano case is significant in the context of family law in India. Shah Bano, a Muslim woman, sought maintenance from her husband after their divorce under Section 125 of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC). The Supreme Court ruled in her favor, granting her the right to maintenance. The case sparked debates on the rights of Muslim women and led to the passage of the Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986, which limited the amount of maintenance a divorced Muslim woman could claim.

Ayodhya Title Dispute Case (2019)

The Ayodhya title dispute is a long-standing and complex legal issue involving the ownership of a religious site in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh. The dispute revolves around a structure claimed by both Hindus and Muslims. In November 2019, the Supreme Court of India delivered a historic judgment, allocating the disputed site to Hindus for the construction of a Ram temple and providing an alternative site for the construction of a mosque for Muslims. The Ayodhya case has had profound implications for religious harmony and the legal landscape in India.

Vodafone Tax Case (2012)

The Vodafone tax case is a significant corporate and taxation dispute. Vodafone, a multinational telecommunications company, acquired the Indian mobile assets of Hutchison Whampoa in 2007.The Indian tax authorities claimed that Vodafone was liable to pay capital gains tax on the transaction, as it involved Indian assets. The case went through various legal proceedings, and in 2012, the Supreme Court of India ruled in favor of Vodafone, stating that the transaction was not taxable in India. The ruling had implications for the taxation of cross-border transactions in the country.
